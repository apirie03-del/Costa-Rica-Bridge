My Story

A Canada–Costa Rica Story That Goes Back Generations

My connection to Costa Rica is not recent. It is deeply rooted in my family history.

Part of my Canadian family moved to Costa Rica in the early 1900s, creating a cross-border story that has continued across generations. Because of that, I do not see Costa Rica as just a destination, and I do not see Canada as separate from that story. Both countries are part of my identity and my family legacy.

I was born in Costa Rica and later moved permanently to Toronto, where I built my life and career in Canada. Living fully in both countries has given me a real understanding of how things work in Costa Rica, as well as the questions, concerns, and expectations Canadians usually have when considering a major move or investment there.

That is why I created Costa Rica Bridge Advisory: to provide Canadians with a more trusted, informed, and practical way to evaluate Costa Rica for travel, relocation, retirement, property, business, or personal matters.

About the Founder

I am Costa Rican-Canadian, bilingual in English and Spanish, and deeply familiar with how both countries operate culturally and practically.

My greatest asset is being able to bridge both worlds: understanding what Canadians need in order to feel safe making big decisions, while also understanding how to navigate local realities in Costa Rica.

I am not a lawyer, accountant, or realtor. My role is to help clients avoid costly mistakes, understand the landscape, and get to the right people with greater confidence.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistakes Canadians Make in Costa Rica

  • Buying property without proper legal review or title verification
  • Trusting unverified agents, advisors, or online information
  • Assuming residency, tax, or corporate rules work the same as in Canada
  • Choosing an area based only on social media or vacation impressions
  • Moving too quickly before understanding healthcare, banking, logistics, and local realities

Most expensive mistakes happen because people move too fast or speak to the wrong person first. The purpose of the advisory is to slow the process down just enough to make it safer, smarter, and more strategic.

If Costa Rica is on your radar, the goal is not only excitement — it is making the right moves with the right people around you.
