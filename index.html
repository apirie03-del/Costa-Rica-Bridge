A Canada–Costa Rica Story That Goes Back Generations

My connection to Costa Rica is not recent. It is deeply rooted in my family history.

Part of my Canadian family moved to Costa Rica in the early 1900s, creating a cross-border story that has continued across generations. Because of that, I do not see Costa Rica as just a travel destination and I do not see Canada as separate from that story. Both countries are part of my identity and my family legacy.

I was born in Costa Rica and later moved permanently to Toronto, where I built my life and career in Canada. Living fully in both countries gave me something most people do not have: a real understanding of Costa Rican culture, how things work locally, and the questions, concerns, and expectations Canadians typically have when they start looking at Costa Rica seriously.

That is why I created Costa Rica Bridge Advisory: to help Canadians move forward with more trust, more clarity, and better local connections when they are considering Costa Rica for travel, relocation, retirement, property, business, or family matters.

About Me

I am Costa Rican-Canadian, bilingual in English and Spanish, and deeply familiar with how both countries operate culturally and practically.

My greatest asset is being able to bridge both worlds: I understand what Canadians need in order to feel safe making big decisions, and I know how to connect them with the right professionals in Costa Rica.

I am not a lawyer, accountant, or realtor. My role is to help clients avoid costly mistakes, understand the landscape, and get to the right people with confidence.

Common Mistakes Canadians Make in Costa Rica

  • Buying property without proper legal review or title verification
  • Trusting unverified agents, advisors, or online information
  • Assuming residency, tax, or corporate rules work the same as in Canada
  • Choosing an area based only on social media or vacations
  • Moving too fast before understanding healthcare, banking, or logistics

Most expensive mistakes happen because people move too quickly or speak to the wrong person first. My role is to help slow the process down just enough to make it safer, smarter, and more strategic.
