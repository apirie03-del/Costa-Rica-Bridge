Our Story

A Canada–Costa Rica story that goes back generations

Costa Rica Bridge Advisory was built on something deeply personal: a family connection between Canada and Costa Rica that reaches back to the late 1800s.

My great-grandfather, Dr. Alexander Fraser Pirie Booth, was a Canadian physician whose journey eventually led him to Costa Rica, where he chose to stay and build a life. What began as a voyage became the beginning of a lasting family story connecting both countries across generations.

That heritage shaped the way I see Costa Rica today: not just as a destination, but as a country best understood through trusted relationships, local context, and careful decisions.

Why this matters

Costa Rica is full of opportunity, but many foreigners move too quickly, trust the wrong people, or make decisions without enough context. This business exists to help clients approach Costa Rica more strategically, with the right questions, introductions, and perspective from the start.

About the Founder

Meet Alexander Pirie

I was born in Costa Rica and later moved permanently to Toronto, where I built my life and career in Canada. Living in both countries has given me a practical understanding of how things work in Costa Rica and the questions international clients often have before making major decisions.

I am Costa Rican–Canadian, trilingual in English, Spanish, and French, and I understand both the cultural and practical gap that often exists between international clients and the realities on the ground in Costa Rica.

Costa Rica Bridge Advisory does not sell legal, immigration, tax, or real estate services directly. Instead, I help clients navigate the country more confidently through independent guidance, local context, and warm introductions to trusted professionals when needed.
