My Story

A Canada–Costa Rica Story That Goes Back Generations

My connection to Costa Rica is not recent. It is deeply rooted in my family history.

Part of my Canadian family moved to Costa Rica in the early 1900s, creating a cross-border story that has continued across generations. Because of that, I do not see Costa Rica as just a destination, and I do not see Canada as separate from that story. Both countries are part of my identity and my family legacy.

I was born in Costa Rica and later moved permanently to Toronto, where I built my life and career in Canada. Living fully in both countries has given me a real understanding of how things work in Costa Rica, as well as the questions, concerns, and expectations Canadians usually have when considering a major move or investment there.

That is why I created Costa Rica Bridge Advisory: to help Canadians move forward with more trust, more clarity, and better local connections when they are considering Costa Rica for travel, relocation, retirement, property, business, or personal matters.

Why this matters

Big Costa Rica decisions usually go wrong when people move too fast, rely on informal advice, or trust the wrong person too early. My role is to make that process safer and smarter.
