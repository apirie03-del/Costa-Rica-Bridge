Our Story

A Canada–Costa Rica connection that goes back generations

Costa Rica Bridge Advisory was built on something deeply personal: a family connection between Canada and Costa Rica that goes back to the late 1800s.

My great-grandfather, Dr. Alexander Fraser Pirie Booth, was a Canadian physician whose journey unexpectedly brought him to Costa Rica, where he chose to remain and build his life.

That story became part of my family’s history and eventually part of my own. Today, Costa Rica Bridge Advisory continues that bridge by helping international clients approach Costa Rica with more clarity, better guidance, and stronger local context.

Why this matters

Costa Rica offers real opportunity, but many foreigners move too quickly, misunderstand how things work, or trust the wrong people too early. This advisory exists to help clients make better-informed decisions from the start.

About the Founder

Meet Alexander Pirie

I was born in Costa Rica and later moved permanently to Toronto, where I built my life and professional career in Canada.

Living across both countries has given me a practical understanding of how Costa Rica is often perceived from abroad, and where international clients most often need better guidance before making major decisions.

I am Costa Rican–Canadian, trilingual in English, Spanish, and French, and I understand both the cultural and practical gap that often exists between international clients and the realities on the ground in Costa Rica.
