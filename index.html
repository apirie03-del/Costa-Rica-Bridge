A Canada–Costa Rica Story That Goes Back Generations

Dr. Alexander Fraser Pirie Booth, my great-grandfather, pictured on horseback — reflecting my family's deep historical connection to Costa Rica.

How the Pirie Family Arrived in Costa Rica

My connection to Costa Rica goes back more than a century and is deeply rooted in my family history.

My great-grandfather, Dr. Alexander Fraser Pirie Booth, was a Canadian physician who left Canada in the late 1880s shortly after graduating from medical school. His original destination was Chile, where he intended to begin his professional career.

During the voyage south along the Atlantic coast of Central America, the ship he was traveling on suffered mechanical problems and was forced to stop near the Caribbean coast of Costa Rica. While waiting for repairs, he decided to explore the country.

Curious about this unfamiliar land, he traveled inland by train and continued the journey on horseback and mule through dense rainforest and mountainous terrain. What was supposed to be a temporary stop turned into something much more meaningful.

The landscape, the people, and the experience of the country left a profound impression on him. Instead of continuing on to Chile, he ultimately chose to remain in Costa Rica and build his life there.

Over time he became a respected physician and an active member of the community, establishing deep personal and professional ties that would connect our family to Costa Rica for generations.

That unexpected journey created a bridge between Canada and Costa Rica that continues through my own life and work today.

Today, Costa Rica Bridge Advisory continues that connection by helping international clients navigate Costa Rica with trusted guidance before making important decisions involving relocation, property, investment, or long-term plans.

About the Founder

My name is Alexander Pirie. I was born in Costa Rica and later moved permanently to Toronto, where I built my life and professional career in Canada.

Living between both countries has given me a deep understanding of how things work in Costa Rica and the questions international clients often have when exploring opportunities there.

I am Costa Rican-Canadian, trilingual in English, Spanish, and French, and deeply familiar with both cultures and systems. My goal is to help clients avoid costly mistakes and connect with the right professionals when needed.

Costa Rica Bridge Advisory does not sell legal or real estate services directly. Instead, I help clients navigate Costa Rica more confidently by serving as a trusted bridge to reliable professionals and local knowledge.
