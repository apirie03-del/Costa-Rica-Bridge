Our Story

A Canada–Costa Rica Story That Goes Back Generations

Costa Rica Bridge Advisory was built on something deeply personal: a multi-generational connection between Canada and Costa Rica.

Dr. Alexander Fraser Pirie Booth, my great-grandfather, pictured on horseback in front of the church in Cartago, reflecting my family’s deep historical connection to Costa Rica.

How the Pirie Family Arrived in Costa Rica

My connection to Costa Rica is not recent. It is deeply rooted in my family history.

My great-grandfather, Dr. Alexander Fraser Pirie Booth, was a Canadian physician whose journey led him to Costa Rica, where he ultimately chose to stay and build a life. What began as a voyage became the foundation of a lasting family story between both countries.

Over time, the Pirie family became part of Costa Rica’s history, particularly in Cartago. That legacy continues to shape my perspective today and is one of the reasons this advisory feels so personal to me.

Costa Rica Bridge Advisory was created to provide Canadians with a trusted way to explore Costa Rica before making major commitments involving relocation, property, investment, or lifestyle changes.

About the Founder

My name is Alexander Pirie. I was born in Costa Rica and later moved permanently to Toronto, where I built my life and career in Canada.

Living in both countries has given me a real understanding of how things work in Costa Rica and the concerns Canadians typically have when exploring opportunities there.

I am Costa Rican–Canadian, trilingual in English, Spanish, and French, with a deep understanding of both Canadian and Costa Rican cultures and systems. My goal is to help Canadians avoid costly mistakes and connect with the right professionals when needed.

Costa Rica Bridge Advisory does not sell legal or real estate services directly. Instead, I help clients navigate Costa Rica more confidently by serving as a trusted bridge to reliable professionals and local knowledge.

Alexander Pirie, founder of Costa Rica Bridge Advisory.
